A husband and wife in counseling today,
A story so common, it happens every day.

The husband wears a polite little smile,
But his rabbit eyes scream, "This may take a while."

The wife wears a grin like she's already won,
She's here to explain what her husband has done.
"We're here to grow, to bloom and to mend..."
Though everyone knows how this story will end.

The counselor sits with years on her side,
She knows who's been hurt and who's swallowed their pride.
She's handled these meetings a thousand times through,
So she's patient and careful in all that she'll do.

She watches a moment... no arguing yet.
That's better than most couples usually get.
Then smiling she asks, "Who would like to begin?"
Everyone knows who's certain to win.

The wife starts talking with plenty to say,
A scroll full of reasons he's failed every day.
A list of improvements from ceiling to floor,
And somehow she always remembers some more.

Two hours go by like a runaway train,
She smiles and says, "I'm just warming my brain."

The counselor sighs, "We've got five minutes more...
Sir, would you like to even the score?"

He opens his mouth with a hopeful delight,
Thinking, "At last... they'll hear my side right."

He barely says, "Well..." before she's back in,
Continuing chapter eleven again.
Another complaint, another review,
Another reminder of things he should do.

The counselor smiles though she's tired inside,
"Next Tuesday?" she asks. "Shall we continue the ride?"

"For now," she says gently, "look deep in your soul."
The wife leaves delighted—mission accomplished, her goal.
The husband leaves silent with plenty to think,
The counselor smiles... and books next week's ink.

Chorus

Ladies, Couples therapy's truly a wonderful plan,
But writing a journal is cheaper than that.

Gentlemen, Read her journal every night before you go to bed,
Fifteen minutes ought to fill your sleepy head.
It won't solve every problem, it won't make your marriage new...
But it's better than a sleeping pill—
And it'll last the whole year through!
